INTRODUCTION¶

The main purpose of this project is to forecast the hourly solar power production in KIVANC 2 GES solar power plant located in Gulnar, Mersin (between 36-37° north latitude and 33-35° east longitude). The forecast is done for the dates between 25th of May and 3rd of June (24 hours of forecast for each day). The data used for every day's production includes the production information until two days before, therefore the data is updated every day in the respective date range. Detailed information about KIVANC 2 and its google earth link is provided below : https://www.kivancenerji.com.tr/kivanc2ges.html https://www.enerjiatlasi.com/gunes/kivanc-2-ges.html https://earth.google.com/web/search/G%C3%BClnar,+Mersin/@36.53293553,33.25506549,1133.49566978a,3417.68721644d,35y,-10.21136707h,0.67542784t,0r/data=CigiJgokCbDmFAthQkNAEbdEevHBMENAGY72bhshJkBAIVzzZJJPBEBA

In the forecasting model, together with the production data of the previous dates, weather information of the respective location is also used. This weather information includes the temperature, relative humidity, downward shortwave radiation flux and cloud cover data. These data are kept in TEMP, REL_HUMIDITY, DSWRF, and CLOUD_LOW_LAYER respectively. TEMP shows the temperature of the location and high temperatures are known to decrease the solar power production efficiency. According to REL_HUMIDITY, the cloudy or rainy days can be identified. In cloudy or rainy days, the production volume decreases. DSWRF indicates the total amount of shortwave radiation including direct and diffuse radiation that reaches the Earth’s surface. CLOUD_LOW_LAYER designates the percentage of cloud cover in the atmosphere for low level cloud types. The weather data are given in long format including the weather information for 9 different coordinates near KIVANC 2 GES solar power plant.

Before starting to modeling part, it is realised that in the given data file some dates were empty in terms of production amount. For the sake of the model, the production amount for these dates are determined by the average of 3 days before and 3 days after values of specified date for each corresponding hour.

After that, to analyse data some visualisation techniques are used. Firstly, total daily production volumes are found and plotted.

Related Literature¶

For this project, course notes are mainly used. Addditionaly, the online text book is used to understand some basics and concept and apply them. Additionally, in this project temperature values are adjusted in some manner. It is known that increasing temparature effects the solar power energy production in positive manner but after some point it decreases the efficiency and amount of energy that is produced. Boston Solar Company publishes an article in their business website. In this article it is said that until 25 degree of celcius increasing temparature effects solar plants in positive manner and after 25 degree of celcius this affect turns to negative. In this project a similar manner is applied. In a data driven manner it is seen that beyond some threshold value total energy produced decreases even the temperature increases. Here, this threshold value is determined and temperature values are swapt with dummy variables. This threshold value is determined by data driven approach and it is 280. Temparatures below 280 is 1 indicates positive impact and temperatures beyond 280 is 0 indicating negative impact.

Conclusion and Future Works¶

Eventhough plots tell that the final model works quite well for the given data, it is obvious this approach and model has disadvantages. First of all, as it is indicated before, the model that is choosen do not follow some properties. The residual analysis shows that the residuals are correlated (espicially at every lag 10) and they are not distributed normal. They are close to be normal but some rules are violated.

Additionally, variable mean_rel is not significant. Probabably, it has important role to explain data but we cannot develop a strategy to reveal that relationship. Same thing has happened with the mean_temparature variable but we could do some explanation following an approach. However, here it is not the case. If this problem can be solved, probably residuals' behaviour also would improve itself and model will be more accurate.

Also, it would better to indicate that models have been constructed in a heuristic manner. First empty dates are filled with 6 days averages. Then, weather variables are averaged and obtained as a single variable. Finally, a threshold for temperature values are determined by data driven and trial and error way. Other researches can determine other rules and obtain different results.

To obtain a better model, some new variables can introduced. For example, daily weather conditions(like sunny,foggy etc.) can obtain and related dummy variables can be introduced. Additionaly, there is no information about the plants conditions and their individual sizes, capacities etc.

Finally, in this project zero values from the data are dropped not to make models confused. Here, some spesific hours will be predicted. Except predicted hours, remaining hours will be automatically zero. In production phase, group1 floowed this manner. Lastly, as it is indicated before, during the prediction phase, models were changed as time passes. However, after some point, group1 decided to use this model with the prediction phase and hin this report this last model is introduced.
